Voter Suppression: Voter ID Laws

---------------

Overly burdensome photo ID requirements block millions of eligible American citizens from voting.1
The stricter the laws on acceptable forms of personal identification, the more difficult it is for minority groups to cast a ballot.2

Voter Suppression: Voter Registration Laws & Purging

---------------

Voter purges are an often-flawed process of cleaning up voter rolls by deleting names from registration lists.3 Voter purging and voter registration hiccups are responsible for at least 17 million people disappearing from voter rolls between 2016 and 2018.4

Voter Suppression: Gerrymandering & Redistricting

---------------

Gerrymandering, the practice of drawing districts to favor one political party, skews election results, makes races less competitive, hurts communities of color, and thwarts the will of the voters. It leads many Americans to feel their voices donâ€™t matter.5

Every 10 years, states re-draw the boundaries of congressional and legislative districts. Redistricting is supposed to reflect changes in population and ensure that everyone is fairly represented.6

Redistricting encourages manipulation of our elections by allowing incumbent politicians to help partisan allies, hurt political enemies and choose their voters before the voters choose them. The current process is used as a means to further political goals by drawing boundaries to protect incumbents and reduce competition, rather than to ensure equal voting power and fair representation. 7
